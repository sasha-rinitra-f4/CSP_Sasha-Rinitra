2 Techniques to help students study better

  • Feynman Technique
  • Pomodoro Technique

The Feynman Technique

The Feynman Technique is a learning method where you explain a concept in simple terms, as if teaching it to a child, identifying knowledge gaps and refining your understanding through simplification and analogies. Here's a breakdown:
1. Choose a Concept: Select a topic you want to learn or improve your understanding of.
2. Explain in Simple Terms: Write down your current understanding of the concept, as if you were explaining it to someone with no prior knowledge.
3. Identify Gaps: As you explain, you'll likely encounter areas where your understanding is weak or incomplete.
4. Review and Refine: Go back to your source material and fill in those knowledge gaps, then rewrite your explanation to be even simpler and more accurate.
5. Simplify and Analogize: Continue to simplify your explanation and use analogies to make the concept easier to grasp and remember.

The Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method based on 25-minute stretches of focused work broken by five-minute breaks. Longer breaks, typically 15 to 30 minutes, are taken after four consecutive work intervals. Each work interval is called a pomodoro. Here's a breakdown:
1. Choose a Task: Select a specific task you want to focus on.
2. Set a 25-Minute Timer: Use a timer (physical or digital) and set it for 25 minutes.
3. Work on the Task: Focus solely on the chosen task for the entire 25 minutes, minimizing distractions.
4. Take a Short Break: Once the timer goes off, take a 5-minute break.
5. Repeat and Longer Break: Repeat steps 1-4 for a total of four "Pomodoros" (25-minute work intervals), then take a longer break (15-30 minutes) before starting another cycle.
